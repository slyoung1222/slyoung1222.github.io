Shannon Young

Shannon comes from many generations of landscape designers, gardeners, and farmers in Pennsylvania, where she grew up. She is grateful that she became a serious gardener in the maritime climate of the Pacific Northwest! As a garden designer, her primary inspiration is traditional English gardens and the juxtaposition of formal outlines softened by informal and naturalistic-inspired plantings. She is particularly fascinated by winter gardens, topiary, spring bulbs, and brickwork. She is the Memberships Director of the Northwest Perennial Alliance (NPA) and a Provisional Consultant in the National Garden Clubs Landscape Design School. A former university administrator and English instructor, Shannon now volunteers on the Board of the Eastside Community Aid Thrift Shop and the NPA. She lives with her husband and young daughter in Kenmore.
